**3. Strategic Analysis (The "So What?"):**
* `**Root Cause:**` The outcome was driven by both plan and execution quality. The plan correctly targeted the parent’s largest performance bottleneck by relaxing compute precision in the GEMM-heavy path without changing routing semantics, and the implementation executed that plan cleanly enough to improve benchmark score. However, the chosen acceleration mechanism did not preserve the parent’s zero-error oracle behavior, so the gain was incremental rather than transformative.
* `**Key Insight:**` The most important lesson is that the lineage can recover meaningful speed without reintroducing full dynamic-FP8 complexity: simply shifting dequantized expert compute from FP32 tensors to BF16 tensors cuts latency materially. But this also reveals that BF16 substitution in GEMM1/GEMM2 is already enough to generate very large artifact error spikes, even when the benchmark’s correctness gate still reports pass. That makes this child valuable as evidence that the main tradeoff frontier is now precision of the dequantized math path, not routing or dispatch structure.
* `**Identified Risk:**` The biggest risk is entering a deceptive local optimum where benchmark-visible correctness stays at 1.0 while hidden numerical margins degrade badly. Because this child improved score despite huge max-error artifacts, future descendants could keep chasing speed through lower-precision eager GEMMs and drift toward a fragile solution that fails under stricter workloads or different evaluator sensitivity.
