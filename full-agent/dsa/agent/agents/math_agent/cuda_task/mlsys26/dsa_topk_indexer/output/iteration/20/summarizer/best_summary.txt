**2. Data-Driven Findings (Facts ONLY):**

**3. Strategic Analysis (The "So What?"):**
*   **Root Cause:** The outcome was driven primarily by plan quality (correctly identifying that a revert was the highest-expected-value move after three consecutive regressions). Execution was faithful but not byte-equivalent to the stated target `dc51d5fc` — hence the 0.047ms latency vs. the grandparent's 0.036ms. Reference baseline noise (2.86ms vs. dc51d5fc's era) may also explain part of the gap.
*   **Key Insight:** **Reverts work, but imperfect reverts leave performance on the table.** The plan explicitly mandated a "byte-equivalent" restoration of `dc51d5fc` (score 9.564, 0.036ms), but the delivered kernel runs ~30% slower than that baseline. Some subtle structural detail — possibly the `cvt_fp8x4_to_float4` helper variant (this child uses a union-cast while `dc51d5fc` may have had a different codegen path), the `compute_scores_kernel`'s shared-memory layout (this child uses `q_smem_u32[64*32]` rather than the transposed `q_smem_f[128][65]` described in the plan's Phase 2.C), or launch-parameter alignment — is not actually matching the champion.
*   **Identified Risk:** **Score ceiling via algorithmic stagnation.** This iteration anchored the floor but did not raise the ceiling. The lineage now has 4 consecutive iterations below 9.564, and reverting again will not unlock new territory. Also risky: the plan's "strict revert" claim was not fully delivered, meaning future planners may mis-estimate the "safe baseline" as 7.97 rather than 9.56.

**4. Actionable Guidance (The "What's Next?"):**
*   **Recommend Stripping:** Perform a *true* byte-equivalent revert by directly resurrecting `dc51d5fc`'s source as the next parent (skip `ab09f5be`). Diff the two `compute_scores_kernel` implementations token-by-token — in particular verify the Q-shared-memory layout (`q_smem_u32` vs transposed-float `q_smem_f[128][65]`) and the inner `h=0..63` loop structure. The 10µs gap between 0.047ms and 0.036ms is almost certainly one of these micro-structural differences.
*   **Recommend Exploration:** Once re-anchored at 9.564, attack the true bottleneck identified earlier — the single-lane accumulation `if (lane_id == 0) total_score += val * weights_smem[h]` inside the `h` loop serializes 64 warp reductions per token. Replace with: (a) per-lane head partitioning (each lane owns a subset of heads, then final warp-reduce across lanes), or (b) CUTLASS/CuTe FP8 WGMMA for the Q·K^T matmul.
*   **Recommend Fusion:** After the matmul bottleneck is removed, revisit TIER-M fusion — but this time use a **register-resident** scoring→sort path (avoid the smem-staging indirection that caused `baa28cba`'s regression). The previously-failed fusion pattern should stay on the blacklist.
*   **Warn:** Do NOT attempt another speculative fusion from this node. The lineage has 4 consecutive sub-champion iterations; the next evolutionary step must be either (a) a clean re-anchor to 9.564 or (b) a structurally new approach (tensor-core MMA), not another incremental tweak on the current 3-tier scaffold.
